The court

Badminton is a sport that can be played in singles or doubles. To play badminton, you need a court 13.40 m long and 5.20 m wide for singles, and 6.10 m wide for doubles. The size of the court changes because in singles, the side lanes don't count.

The shuttlecock

Badminton is played with a plastic or feather shuttlecock. The difference between the two is that a plastic shuttlecock costs less but will have a slower point speed than a feathered shuttlecock.

The match

A badminton match is played in 2 winning sets. A set is won when one side reaches 21 points, but there must be a two-point difference. There can be two possible endings: if one of the two players is at 21 and the other is at 20, then an extra point will be played until one of the two players has a two-point difference. However, there's a limit: if the players reach 29 equal, then the one who scores the point is the winner.

crawl

The crawl is a fast swimming technique involving alternating arm movements and synchronized leg kicks. Swimmers breathe sideways while keeping their face in the water. It's the fastest stroke and the one most commonly used in competition.

Breaststroke

The breaststroke is a swimming technique in which the arms make simultaneous circular movements, while the legs make a "frog-like" beat. Breathing is accomplished by lifting the head out of the water. Breaststroke is known for its characteristic movement of arms and legs and is often preferred for leisure swimming.

Butterfly

Butterfly is a swimming technique characterized by the simultaneous movement of the arms in a butterfly-like trajectory and the undulating stroke of the legs. Swimmers breathe as they lift their heads out of the water. The butterfly is demanding on the muscles and requires precise coordination. It is often practiced in competition for its speed and distinctive style.

Backstroke

The backstroke is a swimming technique characterized by an alternating movement of the arms and legs, while lying on the back. The backstroke is appreciated for its more natural breathing position and is often used in recreational and competitive swimming.
